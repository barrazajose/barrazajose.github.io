Electrical Engineering Program

MISSION

To train professionals in the area of electrical at an engineering degree, complying with the best educational quality standards, capable of providing optimal and sustainable solutions to problems related to the generation, transmission, distribution and use of electrical energy in harmony with the university values and seeking social and environmental well-being; as well as, promoting basic and applied research and impulse technological innovation and linkage to industries.

Faculty of Engineering, UABC.

VISION

In 2030, the Electrical Engineer educational program is widely recognized nationally for being socially responsible, contributing with opportunity, relevance and the best quality standards to the comprehensive training of professionals in electrical engineering. It is an educational program recognized national and international by prestigious evaluating organizations. It promotes, generates, applies, disseminates and transfers knowledge to promote innovation, as well as strengthen the linkage and research, and offering internal services that allow it to be a reference of an educational program in Mexico and Latin America.

Faculty of Engineering, UABC.

Admission Profile

Applicants to enter the Electrical Engineer educational program must have the following knowledge, skills, attitudes and values:

Knowledge:

  • Algebra
  • Trigonometry
  • Analytic Geometry
  • Physics
  • Chemistry

Skills:

  • Interpret physical phenomena through observation
  • Logical thinking
  • Mathematical problem solving
  • Teamwork organization
  • Verbal and written communicative skills

Attitudes:

  • Willing to do Bibliographic and field research
  • Willing to participate in academic, scientific and cultural activities
  • Willing to participate in teamwork
  • Respect for the institution, their teachers and peers
  • Interest in understanding the electrical equipment and systems operation
  • Critical, thoughtful, dynamic and transformative
  • Creativity and imagination

Core Values:

  • Honesty
  • Respect
  • Trust
  • Responsibility
  • Humility
  • Justice
  • Democracy
  • Liberty
  • Loyalty
  • Perseverance
  • Solidarity

Graduate Profile

Upon completion of this program, students should be able to:

  • Identify, formulate and solve complex engineering problems applying the principles of basic science and engineering.
  • Apply, analyze, and synthesize engineering design processes that result in projects that meet specified needs.
  • Develop and conduct adequate experimentation; analyze and interpret data using engineering judgment to draw conclusions.
  • Communicate effectively with different audiences.
  • Recognize their ethical and professional responsibilities in relevant situations in order to make informed judgments considering the impact of engineering solutions in a global, economic, environmental and social context.
  • Recognize the continuing need for additional knowledge and have the ability to locate, evaluate, integrate and apply this knowledge appropriately.
  • Work effectively in teams to set goals, plan tasks, meet deadlines and analyze risks and uncertainties.

Educational objectives

  • Work in public, private, and service companies, related to the design, maintenance, administration and construction of high and low voltage electrical installations.
  • Design, build and operate high and low voltage electrical installations, applying current regulations, respecting the environment with an entrepreneurial attitude.
  • Work in multidisciplinary teams for a better use of electrical energy in a rational and effective way.
  • Critically assess their activities with professionalism, values and ethics, interacting with their peers with respect and valuing human life in their actions.
  • Develop and maintain forms of self-learning through updating and research, assimilating changes in technology in a critical and creative way to improve their professional life.

Occupational Field

Electrical Engineer may develop its professional activities in:

Private sector:

  • Government Institutions.
  • Educational Institutions
  • Research Institutions.
  • Communications and transport field.
  • Public service.

Public Sector:

  • Commercial and service companies.
  • Industrial companies.
  • Educational and research Institutions.

Independent professionals:

  • Business consulting and assessment.
  • Design and optimize control and protections systems for electrical equipment.
  • Select and maintain electrical equipment and material in optimal conditions
  • Diagnosis and evaluation of electrical systems.

Main Projects

  1. Simulation and validation of the electromagnetic model of a transformer through the use of free software of finite elements in 2D and the development of experimental test PRODEP NPTC 2015-2 to 2016-1.
  2. Development of didactic electromagnetic models using the second order MEF-2D UABC UA 2017-1 to 2017-2.
  3. Development of a teaching system for the analysis and/or design of induction machines PRODEP CA 2017-1 to 2018-1.
  4. Development of first order EF-3D nodal software applied to electromagnetism UABC UA 2020-1 to 2020-2.
  5. Proposal and design of a technology for variable caging angle of a low-capacity wind turbine for optimization of electricity generation with code UABC-PTC-526 at the call for the Program for Professional Development of Teachers, Superior Type 2015
  6. Shortage and over-generation problems in micro networks that incorporate renewable energies with code UABC-CA-280 in the 2017 call for the Strengthening of Academic Bodies.
