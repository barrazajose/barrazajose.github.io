What is the Common Core of Engineering?

Gives the student an opportunity to learn about all available careers and take the best vocational decision

The common core of engineering, which started in the Engineering Faculty in 2004-2 cycle, includes a set of subjects that belong to a group of related educational programs from the same area of knowledge, in the basic stage of the career. The common core of engineering is an academic space where students join in a basic training stage, without the degree to which he or she will finally select. Regardless of the career of preference when the student enters the faculty, he enters the common core of engineering, which offers the following benefits:

  1. Allows the attention of a greater number of students, since groups can be integrated and balanced, then the capacity of classrooms can be increased. In this way, the optimal use of available resources is accomplished.
  2. Favors the mobility of students, since they can continue their studies in other academic units through the accreditation of their courses.
  3. Promotes in students basic knowledge, skills, abilities, attitudes and values to solve problems and develop their capabilities, which will allow them to proactively participate in the continuous changes in the different fields of society, such as science, technology, art, sports and culture.
  4. Encourages logical, critical and creative thinking
  5. Allows interpersonal and group relationships with tolerance and respect for cultural diversity.
  6. Promotes optimal performance centered on basic knowledge and skills for permanent self-training.
  7. Stimulates the completion of a community social service associated to different sectors of the community, as an important part of career credits.

The common core of engineering provides the student an opportunity to learn about all the careers available, in order to make the best decision. The academic units will take advantage of this space to strengthen the vocational orientation of the student and thus promote better informed decisions.

Considerations

  • 1.-Once entering the common core, the student will be assigned a tutor who will accompany him/her during his/her stay in the program. This professor will recommend the critical path (career and subject options) in accordance to the professional profile in which the student is interested.
  • 2.-Once the mandatory subjects of the common core of engineering have been credited, the student participates in an automatic auction process for career selection.
  • 3.-The time established to complete the common core of engineering is two semesters.

Learning Units

The learning units that are taught within the common core of engineering are those shown below:

1rst Semester:

  • Diferential calculus
  • Superior Algebra
  • Programming methodology
  • Oral and written communication
  • Introduction to engineering
  • Engineer profesional development
  • English I

2nd Semester:

  • Integral calculus
  • Probability and statistics
  • Vector mechanics
  • Programming and numerical methods
  • English II

Career auction process

  • 1.-Students who have accredited the set of learning units belonging to the common core of engineering may participate in the selection at the auction process to enter into the educational program in which they would like to enroll, in any of the academic units or campus where the curriculum is offered.
  • 2.-The auction process for admission into the educational program of interest for the student will be held based on their academic merits and the allowed spaces availables in the program.
  • 3.-Studens may participate in the auction process to enter the program of their interest at any campus where the program is associated wth the common core of engineering.
  • 4.-The auction process will take place in two stages. In the first stage priority will be given to regular students who have passed the obligatory learning units of the common core of engineerin considering the general grade average obtained. In the second stage irregular students will be included.
