Mechatronics Engineering Program

MISSION

To train professionals in mechatronics engineering field with technical, research and transformative skills. Committed to the community and its environment to propose solutions to problems related to their profession in an effective and efficient way. In addition to being able to integrate into the same in social, cultural, political and productive aspects promoting or generating conditions to be achieved and always seeking compliance with institutional policies and commitments.

Faculty of Engineering, UABC.

VISION

In 2025, the Mechatronic Engineer educational program is widely recognized to be an educational program with national and international recognition where the activities and practices of students and teachers are developed with state-of-the-art technology and a high-level teacher training that promotes teachers and students’ values. Fostering the development of own technology to work projects with productive and social sectors.

Faculty of Engineering, UABC.

Admission Profile

Applicants to enter the Mechatronics Engineer educational program must have the following knowledge, skills, attitudes and values:

Knowledge:

  • Physics
  • Chemistry
  • Computer science

Skills:

  • Teamwork.
  • Verbal and written communicative skills
  • Analyze, interpret and solve problems.

Attitudes:

  • Analytical thinking and tendency to optimization
  • Discipline, order, organization
  • Initiative to undertake research projects
  • Willingness and creative to solve problems
  • Leadership, responsibility and collaboration in teamwork
  • Open mindedness towards changes and scientific, technological and social development
  • Personal and professional improvement and updating search

Core Values:

  • Respect
  • Honesty
  • Tolerance
  • Commitment
  • Perseverance
  • Sense of belonging

Graduate Profile

Upon completion of this program, students will be able to:

  • Identify, formulate and solve complex engineering problems by applying the principles of basic sciences and engineering.
  • Apply, analyze and synthesize engineering design processes resulting in projects that meet specified needs.
  • Develop and conduct appropriate experimentation; analyze and interpret data and use engineering judgment to draw conclusions.
  • Communicate effectively with different audiences.
  • Recognize their ethical and professional responsibilities in situations relevant to engineering and make informed judgments that consider the impact of engineering solutions in the global, economic, environmental, and social contexts.
  • Recognize the permanent need for additional knowledge and have the ability to locate, evaluate, integrate and apply this knowledge appropriately.
  • Work effectively in teams that set goals, plan tasks, achieve deadlines, and analyze risks and uncertainty.

Educational objectives

  • Design and integrate mechatronic systems to provide optimal solutions to social and productive problems with ethics and environmental responsibility.
  • Develop projects to improve industrial processes by implementing and / or updating automation systems to standardize the quality of products and / or services through technical and financial analysis.
  • Understand and value the knowledge obtained through research and training in their discipline to update themselves and acquire emerging tools and techniques applicable to their areas of interest.
  • Integrate interdisciplinary work teams for the development of projects communicating effectively.

Occupational Field

Mechatronic Engineer may work in:

Private sector:

  • Companies that integrate technology and service
  • Manufacturing industry such as:
  • Electric and electronic
  • Automotive
  • Health care
  • Metal mechanic
  • Energies generation industries
  • Food sector
  • Agricultural sector industries

Public Sector:

  • Government agencies and decentralized agencies within the field of mechatronics
  • Trade and industrial development sectors
  • Parastatal industries
  • Communications and transport sector
  • Educational and research Institutions

Independent professionals:

  • Business consulting and assessment

Main Projects

  1. Design and construction of a prototype of a snake robot to search for living beings in collapsed structures. Responsible: Dr. David Isaías Rosas Almeida
  2. Design of a 5 Giga Samples per Second (GSPS) Digital Analog Converter with Optically Activated Sampling based on Fast Switching Photodiodes. Responsible for the project: Dr. Iván Olaf Hernández Fuentes
  3. Design and simulation of a prototype light welder for components on printed circuit boards. Responsible for the project: Dra. Rosa Citlalli Anguiano Cota, Dr. Jesús Rigoberto Herrera García
